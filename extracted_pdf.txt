02 | TRACK B — WHAT WE ARE BUILDING
Why Track B (Optimization Engine)
Track A focuses on predictive modelling — building ML models to forecast quality and energy. Track B
focuses on the optimization engine — using data to find the best batch profiles, store them as golden
signatures, and guide operators in real time. We chose Track B because it plays to our strengths: strong
system design, clear visual storytelling, and the ability to work with our 60-batch dataset without requiring
massive training data.
The GPS Analogy — Understanding Our System
Think of our system exactly like a GPS for factory operations. A GPS does not just show where you
are. It compares your route to the optimal one, recalculates in real-time when you deviate, learns from
traffic patterns, and lets you choose your priority — fastest route vs. least fuel. Our system does
exactly this for manufacturing batches. Current position = live sensor readings during batch Optimal
route = the golden signature Recalculate = deviation detection + correction Your priority = quality
vs. energy preset selection

04 | DOMAIN KNOWLEDGE — PHARMACEUTICAL
STANDARDS
What Industry Are We In?
After analysing the dataset columns — Granulation, Binder Amount, Drying, Compression, Hardness,
Friability, Dissolution Rate, Content Uniformity — we identified this as pharmaceutical tablet
manufacturing. This is a highly regulated industry with established international quality standards (USP,
BP, ICH guidelines). These standards define exactly what "good" looks like for every quality metric.
The Hardness Unit Clarification — An Important Discovery
Issue Found: Our dataset shows Hardness values of 85–110. International pharma standards define
acceptable tablet hardness as 4–10 kg. These seemed incompatible. Resolution: The data is
calibrated to the real world. The unit is Newtons (N), not kilograms. Conversion: 1 kgf = 9.8 N. 85 N ÷
9.8 = 8.67 kgf  Within standard (4–10 kg) 95 N ÷ 9.8 = 9.69 kgf  Within standard (4–10 kg) 110 N ÷
9.8 = 11.2 kgf — Borderline but acceptable Conclusion: The dataset is genuine and accurate. We
will treat Hardness as Newton values and define our "ideal range" based on our own dataset
distribution, not external kg standards.

05 | THE SCORING SYSTEM DESIGN
Chosen Approach: Weighted Scoring + Pareto Visualisation
We evaluated three approaches to turn multiple quality metrics into a comparable score for each batch.
We selected Option 2 (Weighted Scoring) for the core logic, combined with Option 3 (Pareto Visualisation)
for the presentation layer.
Option 1: Pass/Fail Scoring
• Each metric gets 0 or 1 based on whether it hits the standard
• Total score = sum of passes
• Simple but loses nuance — a batch that scores 99% dissolves the same as 85%
Option 2 : Weighted Composite Scoring (OUR CHOICE)
• Each metric normalized to 0-100 scale
• Multiplied by its assigned weight
• Summed to give a final batch quality score 0-100
• Handles nuance — a 99% dissolution scores higher than 85%
Option 3 : Pareto Frontier Visualisation (OUR PRESENTATION LAYER)
• Does not collapse to one score — shows the tradeoff
• Plots Quality Score vs. Energy Consumption for all 60 batches
• Pareto-optimal batches = those where no other batch is better on BOTH dimensions
• Visually powerful for the PDF submission

06 | ENERGY COMPUTATION STRATEGY
Why Energy Is Not In The Production File
The production data file has no energy column. This is not a gap in the data — it is by design. Energy must
be computed from the time-series process data. This is actually one of the strongest aspects of our
system: we genuinely combine both datasets in a meaningful way, not just use one and ignore the other.
The Formula
Total Energy per Batch (kWh) = Sum of all Power_Consumption_kW readings across all phases ÷ 60
Explanation: Power_Consumption_kW gives us kilowatts at each minute. Summing all minutes and
dividing by 60 converts from kW-minutes to kWh. Example — Batch T001: Duration: 210 minutes
Total kW readings summed across all minutes: ~4,582 kW-minutes Total Energy = 4,582 ÷ 60 = 76.4
kWh for that batch
Energy Scoring — How We Convert kWh to a 0-100 Score
Lower energy is better. To make energy comparable to the quality score (which is higher = better), we
invert the energy score. The batch with the lowest energy across all 60 batches gets a score of 100, the
highest gets 0, and all others are scaled linearly between.
Energy Score = 100 × (max_energy - batch_energy) ÷ (max_energy - min_energy)

07 | GOLDEN SIGNATURE FRAMEWORK
Definition
A golden signature is the complete profile of the highest-scoring batch for a given objective preset. It
contains everything needed for an operator to replicate that batch and for the system to detect when a new
batch is deviating from it. Each of our 4 presets has its own golden signature — the "best batch" changes
depending on what you are optimising for.
The Three-Layer Architecture of a Golden Signature
LAYER 1: The Settings Profile
Source: Production data file
The input parameters the operator set before starting the batch.
→ Granulation Time
→ Binder Amount
→ Drying Temperature
→ Drying Time
→ Compression Force
→ Machine Speed
→ Lubricant Concentration
→ Moisture Content
Answers the question: "What did the operator set to achieve this result?"

LAYER 2: The Process Fingerprint
Source: Process data file (aggregated per phase)
The characteristic behaviour of sensors across each of the 8 production phases. Not stored as raw
minute-by-minute data — aggregated to mean/max per phase.
→ Avg Temperature per phase
→ Avg Pressure per phase
→ Avg Motor Speed per phase
→ Avg Compression Force per phase
→ Avg Flow Rate per phase
→ Avg Power Consumption per phase
→ Max Vibration per phase
Answers the question: "What did the machine actually do during this batch?"
LAYER 3: The Outcome Score
Source: Computed from production data + energy calculation
The final quality and energy scores that qualified this batch as golden.
→ Dissolution Rate (30%)
→ Content Uniformity (25%)
→ Friability (20%)
→ Disintegration Time (15%)
→ Hardness (10%)
→ Total Energy (kWh)
→ Composite Quality Score
→ Composite Combined Score
Answers the question: "Why is this batch the golden one?"
WHY ALL THREE LAYERS MATTER: Layer 1 tells an operator what to set before the batch starts.
Layer 2 tells them what the machine should look like during the batch. Layer 3 tells them what
score they need to beat to update the golden signature. Without all three, the system cannot do
real-time guidance.

08 | DEVIATION DETECTION & CORRECTION ENGINE
How Deviation Detection Works
As a new batch runs, its live sensor readings are compared against the golden signature Layer 2 (process
fingerprint) for the current phase. The deviation is expressed as a percentage difference from the golden
signature value for that sensor in that phase.
Deviation (%) = |Live Value − Golden Value| ÷ Golden Value × 100

09 | HUMAN-IN-THE-LOOP DESIGN
Why This Is Our Biggest Differentiator
The problem statement explicitly requires human-in-the-loop functionality. Most competing teams will build
the optimization logic and forget this requirement entirely. Our system treats the operator not as a passive
observer but as an active participant whose decisions shape how the system evolves over time.
The Three Required Human-in-the-Loop Functions
Function 1: Accept or Reject Proposed Changes
• When the correction engine recommends a parameter adjustment, the operator sees it on their
dashboard
• They can Accept — the system logs the acceptance and tracks whether the correction improved
the batch
• They can Reject — the system asks for a reason (free text or dropdown)
• The decision is stored with timestamp, batch ID, phase, and reason
• This is critical — without rejection data, the system cannot learn what does not work in practice
Function 2: Reprioritise Targets Mid-Batch
• At any point during a batch run, the operator can switch the active golden signature preset
• Example: Started on "Best Quality Only" but energy costs are spiking — switch to "Best Quality +
Lowest Energy"
• The system immediately recalculates which golden signature applies and adjusts all deviation
thresholds
• This mid-batch reprioritisation is exactly what the problem statement asks for
• In our prototype this is shown as a preset selector that updates the comparison view live

Function 3: Store Decisions and Reuse
• Every accept/reject decision is written to the decision log with full context

10 | CONTINUOUS LEARNING & DECISION LOG
How Our System Learns Without Complex ML
Continuous learning is a required component of Track B. Our approach does not require deep learning or
retraining models. It uses two simple but powerful mechanisms that together create a system that
genuinely improves over time.
Mechanism 1: Golden Signature Auto-Update
After every completed batch, the system automatically scores it using the same weighted formula. It
then compares that score to the current golden signature score for each preset. If the new batch score
exceeds the current golden signature score: 1. System flags this as a "New Candidate Golden
Signature" 2. Operator is notified on their dashboard 3. Operator can Accept (golden signature
updates) or Reject (keep current) 4. Decision is logged with reasoning 5. If accepted, the new batch
becomes the golden signature for that preset This means the system naturally raises its own bar over
time.
Mechanism 2: The Decision Log
Every human decision made through the system is stored in a structured decision log. This log is the
memory of the system. It does not just record what happened — it records the context, allowing future
batches to benefit from past operator knowledge.
